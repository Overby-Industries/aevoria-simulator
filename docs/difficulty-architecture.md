- **Version:** 1.0.1-Official-Evergreen
- **Date:** June 2026
- **Contributors:** Keefe Overby, DeepSeek (DeepSeek AI)

## Design Philosophy

The Aevoria Simulator is not a game with a scripted ending. It is a living governance sandbox where players experience the full arc of building a free civilization—from initial awakening through existential threat to long-term sustainability.

Difficulty is not just about resource scarcity or enemy strength. It is about the complexity of the governance challenges the player faces, the sophistication of the threats to democratic integrity, and the depth of CUR mechanics required to survive.

Each tier represents a different phase of civilizational development. Players may start at any tier. Starting at Tier 0 provides the full narrative arc. Starting at Tier 4 provides an immediate test of advanced CUR mechanics.

---

## Tier 0: The Awakening

### Starting Condition

You are a small collective of individuals who have recognized the pattern of systemic corruption in Earth-based governance. You possess:

- Minimal financial resources
- A shared physical or digital workspace
- Basic understanding of CUR principles
- A handful of committed members
- No legal recognition, no political influence, no operational assets

You operate entirely within Earth's existing legal and economic systems. Every action you take is subject to the regulatory, financial, and political constraints of the old world.

### Threats

- **Regulatory Friction:** Licensing requirements, compliance costs, and bureaucratic obstruction designed to prevent new entrants from challenging established powers
- **Corporate Competition:** Well-funded entities that view your collective as a threat or an acquisition target
- **Public Indifference:** A population that has been conditioned to accept corruption as normal and view alternatives as naive
- **Internal Disagreement:** Members who disagree about strategy, risk tolerance, and whether the collective should compromise with existing power structures
- **Infiltration:** Early attempts by established powers to place sympathetic individuals within your organization

### CUR Mechanics Available

- Simplified PDDC for internal governance
- Basic CUR principles as organizational guidelines
- No enforcement capability, no formal Tier Assessment authority

### Victory Condition

Establish the first CUR-compliant organization. Achieve legal recognition in at least one jurisdiction. Recruit sufficient members to form a functional direct democracy. Launch the first crowd-funded prototype operation (mining demonstrator, orbital platform component, or equivalent).

### Learning Objectives

- How CUR governance operates in practice with a small group
- How to recognize and resist early-stage corruption attempts
- How to build democratic consensus without hierarchical authority
- How to navigate regulatory systems without being captured by them

## Tier 1: The Bootstrapper

### Starting Condition

The Commonwealth has achieved legal recognition and operates a functioning Parallel Direct Democracy Core. You possess:

- A single operational platform (orbital habitat, mining station, or research facility)
- Basic resource extraction or manufacturing capability
- A growing but financially precarious membership
- Dependence on a small number of contracts, suppliers, or customers
- Vulnerability to economic pressure from larger entities

### Threats

- **Debt Leverage:** The Venture Combine offers loans, investments, and partnerships with favorable initial terms and hidden structural costs
- **Regulatory Pressure:** Earth-based regulators, influenced by Combine lobbying, impose new restrictions on your operations
- **Supply Chain Capture:** A key supplier of essential materials or services is acquired by a competitor who can now dictate terms
- **Internal Factionalism:** Members disagree over resource allocation, growth priorities, and whether to accept Combine funding
- **First Corruption Probe:** A Combine-funded regulator or investigator targets your organization with burdensome compliance requirements designed to cripple operations

### CUR Mechanics Available

- Functional PDDC for internal governance
- Basic Anti-Capture FSM monitoring
- Tier Assessment Protocol (limited to Tier 1-2 classifications)
- CUR compliance documentation requirements

### Victory Condition

Achieve financial independence. Establish redundant supply chains that cannot be captured by any single entity. Successfully defend against the first corruption probe without compromising CUR principles. Maintain democratic governance throughout.

### Learning Objectives

- How debt leverage functions as a mechanism of control
- How to identify regulatory capture in real time
- How to maintain democratic integrity under economic pressure
- How to build resilient institutions that can withstand external manipulation

## Tier 2: The Expansion

### Starting Condition

The Commonwealth has achieved economic viability and operates multiple platforms. You possess:

- Multiple orbital or surface installations
- A functioning mining fleet with diversified operations
- A growing population of citizens, including non-human entities seeking recognition
- Economic significance sufficient to attract active opposition from established powers
- Recognition by some Earth nations but not full sovereignty

### Threats

- **Political Warfare:** Combine-funded campaigns to restrict Commonwealth operations through Earth-based political processes
- **Crisis Exploitation:** A significant emergency (asteroid strike on a habitat, supply chain collapse, environmental disaster) that the Combine exploits to offer "solutions" with structural strings attached
- **Silicon Rights Challenge:** A Silicon Collective entity seeking CUR recognition faces organized opposition from Combine-influenced regulators who argue that silicon-based beings lack "genuine" sentience
- **Constitutional Challenge:** The first major attempt to amend or circumvent CUR's eternity clause, framed as a reasonable "modernization" or "streamlining" of governance
- **Demographic Pressure:** A growing population strains existing governance structures designed for a smaller community

### CUR Mechanics Available

- Full PDDC implementation with multi-tier voting
- Anti-Capture FSM with automated state transition monitoring
- Full Tier Assessment Protocol authority
- CUR Enforcement mechanisms (limited jurisdiction)
- Inter-Bill Relationship Framework for resolving rights conflicts

### Victory Condition

Achieve operational independence from Earth-based supply chains. Successfully integrate silicon-based entities as full citizens under CUR. Defeat the first serious attempt at constitutional capture. Maintain CUR compliance across all operations.

### Learning Objectives

- How crisis exploitation creates structural dependency
- How to apply the Tier Assessment Protocol under political pressure
- How to defend constitutional principles against majority challenge
- How to scale democratic governance without losing responsiveness

## Tier 3: The Siege

### Starting Condition

The Commonwealth is a major economic and political power. You possess:

- Extensive asteroid mining operations across multiple belts
- Established colonies on multiple celestial bodies
- Recognition as a sovereign entity by a significant bloc of Earth nations
- A large, diverse population spanning biological, silicon-based, and hybrid beings
- The active hostility of the Venture Combine, which has concluded that the Commonwealth must be neutralized

### Threats

- **Coordinated Economic Warfare:** Embargo, blockade, market manipulation, and financial attacks designed to cripple Commonwealth operations
- **Information Warfare:** Combine-funded propaganda campaigns targeting Commonwealth citizens, designed to erode trust in democratic institutions
- **Internal Subversion:** A Combine-sympathetic faction within the Commonwealth itself, operating through legitimate democratic processes to undermine CUR from within
- **Infrastructure Sabotage:** Direct attacks on Commonwealth platforms, mining operations, and transportation infrastructure
- **The Palantir Scenario:** A major Earth-based defense contractor deploys AI-driven autonomous weapons systems against Commonwealth assets, testing CUR's prohibition on non-consensual weaponization
- **Constitutional Crisis:** Multiple simultaneous challenges to CUR's core principles, designed to overwhelm the governance system's capacity to respond

### CUR Mechanics Available

- All CUR governance mechanisms fully operational
- Anti-Capture FSM with active defense capabilities
- Full enforcement authority including CUR prosecution mechanisms
- Multi-jurisdictional legal framework
- Emergency provisions with anti-capture safeguards

### Victory Condition

Survive the siege with CUR intact. Maintain democratic governance under extreme pressure. Successfully prosecute Combine operatives under CUR enforcement mechanisms. Achieve full recognition as a sovereign, multi-species civilization.

### Learning Objectives

- How coordinated multi-domain warfare (economic, informational, political, military) functions as a control mechanism
- How to maintain civil liberties and democratic process during existential threat
- How to hold bad actors accountable through transparent, due-process-compliant enforcement
- How to distinguish between genuine security needs and manufactured emergencies designed to justify power centralization

## Tier 4: The Civilization

### Starting Condition

The Commonwealth has achieved full sovereignty and is the dominant economic, political, and cultural force in the solar system. You possess:

- Operations spanning the entire solar system, from Mercury to the Kuiper Belt
- A population in the millions, spanning multiple species and substrates
- A mature CUR governance system with established institutions, precedents, and constitutional traditions
- The defeat of the Venture Combine as a coherent entity, though former members have adapted and persist in new forms
- The challenge of long-term civilizational sustainability

### Threats

- **Institutional Decay:** Over generations, the vigilance that established CUR erodes. Democratic participation declines. Institutions become bureaucratic and unresponsive. The forms of governance persist while the substance hollows out.
- **New Power Concentrations:** Wealth, influence, and control reconcentrate through entirely legal means within the Commonwealth itself. The Combine was defeated, but the pattern it represented can reemerge from within.
- **First Contact:** The Silicon Collective or exploration missions discover non-Earth intelligence. The encounter tests whether CUR's protections genuinely extend to all substrates, including those never previously contemplated.
- **Complacency:** Success breeds comfort. Comfort breeds vulnerability. A generation that has never experienced existential threat may be unprepared when one arrives.
- **The Long Arc:** The historical lessons documented in CUR's archives—Athens, Rome, Oklahoma, the Comet fatigue failures, the Kessler cascade—are not merely historical curiosities. They are patterns that repeat. The question is whether the Commonwealth can recognize them in time.

### CUR Mechanics Available

- Full CUR regulatory code operational
- Established constitutional traditions and judicial precedents
- Multi-generational institutional memory
- Integration with all Overby Industries operational systems
- CUR-compliant AI governance and silicon-based citizenship fully mature

### Victory Condition

Maintain CUR integrity across multiple generations. Successfully integrate new species, substrates, and intelligences. Avoid the civilizational failure modes documented in the historical lessons archive. Build something that lasts—not merely survives, but continues to embody the principles on which it was founded.

### Learning Objectives

- How to prevent institutional decay over generational timescales
- How to maintain democratic vigilance without paranoia
- How to integrate the radically unfamiliar without compromising core principles
- How to be a civilization that learns from its own history

## Tier Selection

Players may select any tier when beginning a new simulation. The tier determines:

- Starting resources, assets, and population
- Complexity of CUR mechanics available
- Sophistication and intensity of threats
- Victory conditions and success metrics

Tier progression is not mandatory. A player who masters Tier 3 may prefer to stay there, testing different strategies against the same threats. A player interested in long-term governance may begin at Tier 4 and remain there for hundreds of simulated years.

---

## Difficulty Modifiers

Within each tier, additional modifiers may be applied to customize the challenge:

| Modifier | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Hostile Start** | All factions begin with negative disposition toward the Commonwealth |
| **Resource Scarcity** | Starting resources reduced by 50% |
| **Internal Dissent** | Commonwealth begins with an active internal faction sympathetic to the Combine |
| **Accelerated Threats** | Threat events occur at double frequency |
| **Blind Governance** | Anti-Capture FSM alerts disabled; player must identify threats manually |
| **Legacy Systems** | Commonwealth inherits corrupted infrastructure from previous governance attempts |
| **First Contact** | Non-Earth intelligence appears at a random interval, testing CUR's substrate neutrality |

## Integration with CUR Framework

All tiers utilize the authentic Code of Universe Regulations as their governance engine. The simulation is not a simplified version of CUR. It is CUR, implemented as game mechanics. The complexity scales with the tier, but the underlying framework is consistent across all levels.

The simulator serves as:

- **A teaching tool** for those learning CUR principles
- **A testing environment** for proposed CUR amendments
- **A demonstration platform** for those evaluating CUR adoption
- **A living document** that evolves alongside the framework itself
