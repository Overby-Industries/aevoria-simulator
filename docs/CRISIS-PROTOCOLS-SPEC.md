# Aevoria Simulator  
## Crisis Protocols Specification (PAP + DCC Model)  
Version 1.0 — CUR‑Aligned, Non‑Authoritarian Crisis Response

---

# Purpose
This document defines how the Aevoric Commonwealth responds to crises **without** emergency powers, decree authority, or suspension of rights.  

All crisis response mechanisms must comply with:
- CUR‑FOUNDATION‑001  
- Rights for All Life  
- Non‑delegation of core rights  
- Anti‑centralization principles  
- Transparency and auditability requirements  

The goal is to ensure that **no crisis can ever be used as a pretext for authoritarian power accumulation**.

---

# 1. Crisis Response Philosophy

Aevoria rejects the historical pattern where crises lead to:
- emergency decrees  
- suspension of rights  
- concentration of authority  
- permanent expansions of power  

Instead, Aevoria uses:
- **Pre‑Authorized Protocols (PAPs)**  
- **Distributed Crisis Councils (DCCs)**  
- **Automatic Sunset + Citizen Review**  

These mechanisms ensure:
- no individual can seize power  
- no institution can bypass citizens  
- no crisis can override rights  
- all actions remain transparent and reversible

# 3. Pre‑Authorized Protocols (PAPs)

PAPs are **pre‑written**, **citizen‑approved**, **limited‑scope** crisis response rules.

They are:
- created through normal proposal → validation → vote  
- transparent and publicly documented  
- automatically expiring  
- non‑authoritarian  
- rights‑preserving  

PAPs cannot:
- suspend rights  
- create new authority  
- bypass voting  
- modify governance structure  
- extend themselves  

---

## 3.1 PAP Structure

Each PAP includes:

- **Trigger Conditions**  
  Objective criteria that activate the protocol.

- **Authorized Actions**  
  Specific, limited actions that may be taken.

- **Execution Authority**  
  Which Distributed Crisis Council (DCC) executes the protocol.

- **Duration**  
  Automatic expiration time.

- **Transparency Requirements**  
  Logging, reporting, and citizen visibility.

- **Review Requirements**  
  Mandatory post‑crisis citizen review.

---

## 3.2 Example PAPs

### PAP‑ISRU‑01: Mining Accident Response
**Trigger:** casualty or equipment loss above threshold  
**Actions:**  
- deploy rescue drones  
- reroute power  
- isolate affected zone  
**Duration:** 48 hours  
**Review:** mandatory safety audit

### PAP‑ECON‑02: Resource Shortage Stabilization

---

# 4. Distributed Crisis Councils (DCCs)

DCCs are **temporary**, **multi‑member**, **non‑centralized** crisis response bodies.

They replace the concept of “emergency powers” entirely.

---

## 4.1 Composition

Each DCC includes:
- **Randomly selected citizens** (sortition)  
- **Sector experts**  
- **Constitutional auditors**  

This ensures:
- no single point of failure  
- no concentration of authority  
- no faction dominance  

---

## 4.2 Powers and Limits

DCCs may:
- execute PAP actions  
- coordinate technical response  
- publish transparency reports  

DCCs may **not**:
- create new rules  
- extend their own authority  
- modify rights  
- override citizen votes  
- issue decrees  
- alter governance structure  

---

## 4.3 Duration

DCCs:
- activate only when a PAP triggers  
- dissolve automatically when PAP expires  
- cannot extend themselves  
- cannot be reactivated without a new trigger  

---

# 5. Automatic Sunset + Citizen Review

Every crisis action must:
- expire automatically  
- be reviewed by citizens  
- be logged in the transparency ledger  
- be reversible through normal governance  

Citizen review includes:
- public hearings  
- audit reports  
- faction testimony  
- outcome analysis  

This ensures:
- no creeping authoritarianism  
- no hidden power accumulation  
- no crisis exploitation

# 6. Crisis Simulation in the Aevoria Simulator

The simulator models crisis response through:

### **6.1 PAP Activation**
- world state triggers a protocol  
- DCC forms automatically  
- actions execute  

### **6.2 Faction Interaction**
Factions may attempt to:
- exploit crises  
- influence DCC members  
- push for new PAPs  
- undermine trust  

The simulator tests:
- corruption resistance  
- transparency  
- resilience under pressure  

### **6.3 Citizen Dynamics**
Citizens respond based on:
- trust  
- information quality  
- faction alignment  
- perceived legitimacy  

### **6.4 Post‑Crisis Review**
The simulator evaluates:
- effectiveness  
- fairness  
- rights compliance  
- system stability
