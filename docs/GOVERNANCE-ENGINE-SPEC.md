# Aevoria Simulator  
## Governance Engine Specification  
Version 1.0.1 — CUR‑Aligned, Non‑Authoritarian Design

---

# Purpose
The Governance Engine models the Aevoric Commonwealth’s political and legal systems as defined by the Code of Universe Regulations (CUR) and the Progressive Direct Democracy Constitution (PDDC).  

This engine is designed to:
- simulate direct democracy at scale  
- test constitutional resilience  
- expose corruption vectors  
- validate CUR compliance  
- model distributed crisis response without emergency powers  

The Aevoria Simulator must **never** allow:
- emergency decrees  
- suspension of rights  
- concentration of authority  
- unilateral executive action  

These are explicitly forbidden by CUR‑FOUNDATION‑001.

---

# 1. Core Principles (from CUR Foundations)

The Governance Engine is built on the following principles:

### **1. Non‑Delegation of Core Rights**
No individual or office may override, suspend, or assume the rights of citizens.

### **2. No Emergency Powers**
Crises do not justify authoritarian shortcuts.  
All crisis responses must follow pre‑approved protocols.

### **3. Transparency & Auditability**
All governance actions are logged, visible, and reviewable.

### **4. Citizen Sovereignty**
Citizens are the ultimate authority.  
Institutions exist to execute their will, not replace it.

### **5. Anti‑Capture Architecture**
No faction, wealth bloc, or institution may accumulate unchecked influence.

### **6. Rights for All Life**
All proposals must pass a rights‑compliance check before entering deliberation.

---

# 2. Governance Engine Components

The engine consists of six major subsystems:

1. **Proposal System**  
2. **CUR Compliance Validator**  
3. **Deliberation & Influence Model**  
4. **Voting System (Direct Democracy)**  
5. **Implementation System**  
6. **Distributed Crisis Response (No Emergency Powers)**  

Each subsystem is modular and interacts through well‑defined interfaces.

---

# 3. Proposal System

A **Proposal** is the fundamental governance object.

### Proposal Types
- **Legislative Proposal** — new law or policy  
- **Budget Allocation** — treasury changes  
- **Regulatory Adjustment** — ISRU, safety, environment  
- **Constitutional Amendment** — PDDC modification  
- **Protocol Activation** — crisis response protocol  
- **Sector Council Directive** — technical or operational rule  

### Proposal Lifecycle
1. **Drafting** — citizen, faction, or institution authors proposal  
2. **Submission** — proposal enters the queue  
3. **CUR Validation** — legality and rights compliance  
4. **Deliberation** — influence, debate, coalition formation  
5. **Voting** — direct democracy  
6. **Implementation** — system updates world state  
7. **Review** — citizens evaluate outcomes  

---

# 4. CUR Compliance Validator

Every proposal must pass a multi‑stage validation:

### **1. Rights for All Life Check**
Does the proposal violate any fundamental rights?

### **2. Non‑Delegation Check**
Does it attempt to create:
- emergency powers  
- decree authority  
- suspension of rights  
- centralized control  

If yes → **automatically rejected**.

### **3. Threshold Check**
Does the proposal trigger:
- environmental thresholds  
- safety thresholds  
- governance thresholds  
- economic thresholds  

If triggered → requires additional review.

### **4. Conflict Check**
Does it conflict with existing laws or protocols?

### **5. Constitutional Check**
Does it modify PDDC structures?  
If yes → requires supermajority.

---

# 5. Deliberation & Influence Model

Before voting, proposals enter a deliberation phase.

### Influence Sources
- citizen discussion  
- faction lobbying  
- media campaigns  
- data transparency  
- coalition formation  
- whistleblowing  

### Influence Constraints
- no coercion  
- no vote buying  
- no suppression of information  
- no monopolization of communication channels  

Violations trigger:
- penalties  
- loss of influence  
- legal consequences  

---

# 6. Voting System (Direct Democracy)

Aevoria uses **pure direct democracy** with optional **liquid delegation**.

### Voting Rules
- one citizen = one vote  
- no weighted votes  
- no privileged classes  
- no veto authority  

### Voting Types
- **Simple Majority** — standard proposals  
- **Supermajority (⅔ or ¾)** — constitutional amendments  
- **Threshold Majority** — proposals affecting safety/environment  

### Delegation Rules
- citizens may delegate votes  
- delegation is revocable at any time  
- delegates cannot accumulate permanent power  
- delegates cannot re‑delegate  

---

# 7. Implementation System

If a proposal passes:

- laws update  
- treasury adjusts  
- sector councils receive directives  
- ISRU rules change  
- institutions reconfigure  
- metrics update  

All changes are:
- logged  
- auditable  
- reversible through future proposals  

---

# 8. Distributed Crisis Response (No Emergency Powers)

Aevoria does **not** use emergency powers.  
Instead, it uses **three non‑authoritarian mechanisms**:

---

## 8.1 Pre‑Authorized Protocols (PAPs)

Protocols are:
- written in advance  
- approved by citizens  
- limited in scope  
- automatically expiring  

Examples:
- mining accident response  
- habitat breach response  
- economic shock stabilization  
- misinformation containment  

Protocols cannot:
- suspend rights  
- create new authority  
- bypass voting  
- modify governance structure  

---

## 8.2 Distributed Crisis Councils (DCCs)

Temporary councils formed by:
- randomly selected citizens  
- sector experts  
- constitutional auditors  

DCCs:
- execute pre‑authorized protocols  
- cannot create new rules  
- cannot extend their own authority  
- dissolve automatically  

---

## 8.3 Automatic Sunset + Citizen Review

All crisis actions:
- expire automatically  
- require post‑crisis review  
- cannot be extended without a vote  
- cannot modify rights or governance  

This ensures:
- no power accumulation  
- no “temporary” dictatorship  
- no loopholes  

---

# 9. Institutional Interactions

### Institutions include:
- Citizen Assembly  
- Sector Councils

- Distributed Crisis Councils (temporary)

- no institution may accumulate unilateral authority  
- all institutions are transparent

The Governance Engine actively tests:
- corruption resistance

- transparency systems

---

# 12. Purpose of the Governance Engine

The Governance Engine is the **constitutional heart** of the Aevoria Simulator.

It exists to:
- test CUR under stress  
- validate PDDC mechanics  
- expose corruption vectors  
- model distributed crisis response  
- ensure no authoritarian loopholes  
- preview Aevoria’s future society  

Aevoria survives not through power, but through **protocols, transparency, and the sovereignty of its citizens**.
